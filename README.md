# Exploratory Data Analysis: Heart Disease Prediction

## Overview
This project presents an exploratory data analysis of a heart disease dataset, focusing on identifying patterns and relationships between medical attributes and the likelihood of cardiovascular disease.

The analysis aims to support early detection by understanding key risk factors and their influence on heart disease outcomes.

---

## Definition
Cardiovascular disease (CVD), commonly referred to as heart disease, includes a range of conditions affecting the heart and blood vessels. These conditions may lead to serious complications such as heart attacks, strokes, and other life-threatening health issues.

---

## Problem Statement
Heart disease remains one of the leading causes of death globally, responsible for more than 20 million deaths annually. Despite ongoing efforts to control risk factors such as high blood pressure and unhealthy diets, identifying individuals at high risk remains a challenge.

Early detection plays a critical role, as it is estimated that up to 80% of premature heart attacks and strokes could be prevented with timely intervention. Leveraging data analysis techniques can help predict potential risk cases and support better healthcare decision-making.

---

## Objective
The objective of this project is to explore and analyze the dataset to:
- Identify key factors associated with heart disease  
- Understand relationships between medical variables  
- Discover patterns that may indicate higher risk levels  
- Support data-driven insights for early detection  

---

## Dataset Description
The dataset contains medical and clinical attributes related to patients, including:

- Demographic information (e.g., age, sex)  
- Clinical measurements (e.g., blood pressure, cholesterol levels)  
- Health indicators (e.g., chest pain type, heart rate)  
- Target variable indicating the presence or absence of heart disease  

---

## Methodology

### Data Cleaning
- Checked for missing values and inconsistencies  
- Ensured data types and formats were appropriate  

### Exploratory Data Analysis
- Analyzed distributions of key variables  
- Explored relationships between features and target variable  
- Identified potential risk indicators  

### Visualization
- Created visualizations to highlight patterns and trends  
- Compared different groups to understand variations in risk  

---

## Key Findings

- Certain medical attributes showed strong relationships with heart disease presence  
- Age and clinical indicators played a significant role in risk variation  
- Some variables demonstrated clear separability between patients with and without heart disease  
- Patterns observed suggest that combining multiple features improves understanding of risk  

---

## Conclusion

The analysis provided meaningful insights into the factors influencing heart disease. By identifying key variables and their relationships, the project highlights the importance of data-driven approaches in early detection and prevention.

Further work could involve building predictive models to enhance accuracy and support clinical decision-making.
